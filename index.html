Project Inspiration

The inspiration behind creating my portfolio website stems from a desire to showcase my skills and projects in a way that reflects my growth as a developer. As I progressed through my studies, I realized the importance of having an online presence—a space where I could display my work, share my learning process, and connect with others in the tech community. It is is more than just a requirement for the Holberton School project; it's a personal milestone. It allows me to present my projects visually and interactively, highlighting both technical skills and creativity. This process has also given me hands-on experience with web development tools and technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and a better understanding of how to build responsive, user-friendly interfaces. Additionally, by creating a portfolio website, I hope to bridge the gap between my academic experience and the professional world. It serves as a foundation for my future career, giving potential employers or collaborators a chance to see the work I am passionate about. Ultimately, the project reflects my journey in tech and my commitment to continuous learning and improvement.

Features

Feature 1

This is the home page of my portfolio website. It has a brief description of myself and what I do. I have also added few animations to add to it's interactivity and make it more appealing to the user/visitors.
In addition, I have added icons linking to my social media profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub, Twitter), providing users with alternative ways to connect with me. Also there is a link to my CV where I have layed out my skills and projects.

Feature 2

In this section, is where i have listed my coding and professional skills. For each skill, I incorporated a progress bar to visually indicate my proficiency level. The progress bars offer an intuitive way for visitors to gauge my expertise in a glance.
The length of each progress bar corresponds to a percentage, representing my confidence or experience with the skill.
The combination of the column structure and progress bars keeps the design minimal yet functional, making the section aesthetically pleasing and easy to understand.

Feature 3

The section begins with a bold heading inviting users to get in touch with me.
I've included a contact form with fields for the user’s name, email, subject, and message. The form is minimalistic but functional, ensuring it’s easy to fill out.
Each input field is clearly labeled to guide the user, and I have implemented basic form validation to ensure the correct information is submitted.
